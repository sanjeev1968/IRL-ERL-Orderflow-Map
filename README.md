 IRL ERL Orderflow Map

IRL ERL Orderflow Map is a free and open-source Pine Script indicator for TradingView.

This indicator is designed to help traders visually map Internal Range Liquidity (IRL) and External Range Liquidity (ERL) using general orderflow, imbalance, and liquidity concepts.

The main purpose of this project is to make liquidity-based chart mapping accessible to traders without relying on paid or private indicators.

---

## What Is IRL and ERL?

### Internal Range Liquidity (IRL)

Internal Range Liquidity refers to internal price areas where price may return before continuing toward an external objective.

In this indicator, IRL can be represented through imbalance-style zones such as:

- Fair Value Gaps
- Breakaway Gaps
- Internal reaction zones
- Premium or discount imbalance areas

These areas are not direct buy or sell signals. They are visual reference zones for studying how price may react inside a range.

### External Range Liquidity (ERL)

External Range Liquidity refers to liquidity resting outside the current internal range.

Examples include:

- Swing highs
- Swing lows
- Previous day high and low
- Previous week high and low
- Previous month high and low

These levels help traders visually identify possible external liquidity objectives.

## Purpose of the Indicator

The indicator is built around one simple idea:

Price often reacts from internal liquidity areas and may later move toward external liquidity.

This tool helps organize that information visually by marking internal zones, external liquidity references, swing points, higher-timeframe levels, and active orderflow context.

It is not designed to predict price.

It is not designed to provide automatic buy or sell signals.

It is a visual liquidity mapping tool for traders who study market structure and orderflow.

## How The Components Work Together

This script combines multiple liquidity tools into one workflow.

The components are not meant to work as separate unrelated indicators. They are designed to support the same purpose: mapping the relationship between internal liquidity and external liquidity.

### Internal Zones

Fair Value Gaps and Breakaway Gaps are used as visual internal liquidity zones.

These zones help traders identify areas where price may return, rebalance, or react.

### External Liquidity Levels

Swing highs, swing lows, and previous high/low levels are used as external liquidity references.

These levels help traders identify possible areas where price may seek liquidity.

### Swing Markers

Swing markers provide visual reference points for important highs and lows.

They help users see where external liquidity may be resting.

### Session Ranges

Session ranges help traders observe how price behaves during major market sessions.

They can be useful for studying liquidity creation, liquidity sweeps, and session-based movement.

### Dashboard

The dashboard provides a quick visual summary of active liquidity context across selected timeframes.

It helps traders avoid switching between multiple charts just to check basic orderflow context.

## Suggested Workflow

A simple workflow for using this indicator:

1. Start from the higher timeframes such as Monthly, Weekly, and Daily.
2. Identify active internal liquidity zones.
3. Observe nearby external liquidity reference levels.
4. Drop to 4H, 1H, or 15M for refinement.
5. Use your own confirmation model before considering any trade.
6. Manage risk properly on every setup.

The indicator should be used as an analysis tool, not as a complete trading system.
